Art Class Pre-enrollment Procedures (Before Registration)
Students who have officially declared the Studio Art major or
minor through the Registrar’s website may request pre-
enrollment in Studio Art classes. This process helps students:
• Select courses based on their interest and degree plan
• Stay on track to graduate in four years
Pre-enrollment requires advance preparation. All steps below must
be completed before the announced pre-enrollment date (shared
each semester closer to the time).
Steps to Pre-enroll
1. Declare Your Major or Minor
Submit your Studio Art major or minor declaration on the
Registrar’s webpage.
2. Plan Your Schedule
Meet with your assigned primary academic advisor to create a
schedule with no time conflicts.
3. Get Advisor Clearance
Advisors must clear you for registration before the pre-enrollment
date. Confirm this clearance at the end of your advising session.
4. Clear All Holds
In Self-Service, ensure you have no holds on your account,
including:
• Handbook agreement
• Payment terms agreement

5. Complete the Studio Art Pre-enrollment Google Form
On the designated date and time, fill out the Google Form sent by
the Department Chair. The form will include a list of courses
offered for the upcoming semester, and you will make your
selections directly on the form. You will need to provide:
• Your student ID number
• Confirmation that all holds are cleared
• Confirmation that your requested courses do not conflict with other
planned courses
How to Confirm Your Enrollment
• Check the Course Plan tab in Self-Service for your schedule.
• Allow time for the Registrar’s Office to process enrollments after
registration begins.
• Once pre-enrolled, you cannot change your class until the add/
drop period at the end of registration.
Degree Plans and Planning Tools:
Degree Plans for both the Bachelor of Arts (BA) and Bachelor
of Fine Arts (BFA) in Studio Art are available on the Registrar’s
webpage.
• The BFA is by application only—interested students should contact
the Department Chair during their sophomore or junior year.
