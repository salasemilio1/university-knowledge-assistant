### 3B. BILLING & PAYMENT

**Billing System:** SU uses electronic billing (e-bills) through the Transact Payment Portal, accessed via Self-Service. Fall semester bills are generated after registration and financial aid awards are finalized; a reminder bill is sent mid-July. Spring semester bills are generated in November.

Students must accept the Southwestern University Payment Terms Agreement at registration; declining blocks enrollment.

**Payment Options:**
1. In person at Business Office (Cullen Building, 1st floor): cash, checks, money orders, cashier's checks
2. By mail (checks, money orders, cashier's checks): SU, 1001 E University Ave, Georgetown, TX 78626
3. Online via Transact Payment Portal:
   - Credit/debit: 2.95% processing fee (minimum $3 for payments between $20–$106)
   - ACH/electronic check: currently no fee (subject to change)
   - SU Payment Plan: interest-free installments (up to 5 per term); $25 non-refundable enrollment fee

**Parent/Authorized User Access:** Students must send a "Payer Invitation" through the Transact portal to give parents or others access to view bills, make payments, or enroll in the payment plan. FERPA prohibits releasing student financial information without written student authorization.

**Late Payment Consequences:**
- Initial $150 late payment fee for accounts with no payment arrangement by the payment deadline
- Students with past-due balances may be dropped from all classes
- Students with past-due balances cannot move into on-campus housing for fall
- Pre-registration hold placed when estimated funds/credits are not covering an unpaid balance of $300 or more; transcript access is also blocked during hold

**Non-Payment Course Removal:**
- Cancellation (before first day): full refund of tuition/fees paid; all financial aid canceled; may re-register before end of add period
- Financial withdrawal (on or after first day): student responsible for applicable tuition percentage per refund schedule; room charges not refunded; financial aid subject to reduction

**Delinquent Accounts:** Accounts remaining delinquent after semester end are referred to collection agencies and reported to national credit bureaus. Student is responsible for all collection fees, attorney fees, and court costs. Student account balances are considered educational loans and are NOT dischargeable in bankruptcy.

### 3D. FINANCIAL AID PROGRAMS

**Overview:** SU administered $66+ million in financial aid in 2024–25. All need-based aid requires annual FAFSA submission. FAFSA priority deadline: **March 1** (available at www.studentaid.gov starting October 1 for the following academic year).

**Federal Programs:**
- Federal Pell Grant
- Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)
- Federal Direct Stafford Loan (subsidized and unsubsidized)
- Federal College Work-Study
- Federal Direct PLUS Loan

**State of Texas Programs:**
- Tuition Equalization Grant (TEG) — note: may have higher or different SAP standards than baseline SU policy; contact Financial Aid Office for specifics
- College Access Loan

**Institutional Aid:**
- Merit scholarships (based on academic merit or fine arts talent; criteria and amounts vary by program)
- Need-based grants and scholarships
- Work opportunities (part-time employment in University offices, library, residence halls, dining)
- Loans and financing options

**Full-Time Enrollment Requirement:** All University scholarships and grants require full-time enrollment. If a student enrolls part-time, University scholarship/grant is canceled for that semester. Exceptions (both result in prorated aid):
1. Graduating seniors taking only enough courses to finish their degree
2. Students approved for part-time attendance for medical reasons (request through Center for Academic Success)

**Over-Award Rule:** Combined aid cannot exceed total cost of education. SU institutional aid cannot exceed institutional tuition charges. No University funds are disbursed as cash.

**Texas Guaranteed Tuition Plan (formerly Texas Tomorrow Fund):** Families may apply these funds toward tuition. Must provide plan ID card copies to both Financial Aid Office and Business Office.

### 3E. SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP) FOR FINANCIAL AID

SAP is reviewed at the end of each academic year using cumulative records.

**Qualitative Standard:** Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 (SU coursework only)

**Quantitative Standard (Completion Rate):** Must successfully pass 80% of all credits attempted at SU.
- Courses dropped before the record-entry period: NOT counted as attempted
- Courses dropped after the record-entry period: counted as attempted but NOT completed (hurts completion rate)
- CR grade = successfully completed (counted in both numerator and denominator)
- NC grade = not successfully completed (counted only in denominator)
- Incomplete grade = counted against completion rate for the evaluation period; does not affect GPA until resolved
- Failed courses = counted toward attempted, not completed; affects GPA
- Repeated courses = most recent satisfactory grade counts for GPA; all attempts count toward credits attempted/earned

**Maximum Timeframe:** 175 credits maximum to complete a degree (includes ALL transfer credits and all enrollment periods, even those without financial aid). Exceeding 175 credits = financial aid ineligibility.

**Transfer Students:** Considered in good standing for their first academic year at SU; reviewed at end of first academic year.

**Readmitted Students:** Must meet SAP standards at time of readmission; students who left in ineligible status must restore SAP before receiving aid.

**Failure and Appeal Process:**
1. Student declared ineligible; written notification sent
2. Financial aid rescinded for following semester
3. Student may appeal in writing to the Financial Aid Appeals Committee (Dean of Financial Aid, AVP for Admission, Senior Director of Center for Academic Success and Advising, AVP for Academic Affairs or designee)
4. Appeal must include: (a) reasons for failure and (b) what has changed to allow future progress
5. Approved appeals result in one semester of probation with academic plan; eligible for aid during probation
6. After probation: must meet SAP OR be on track with the Committee's academic plan to continue
7. Summer coursework may restore eligibility by the following fall semester

**SAP and Withdrawal:** If a student withdraws while on financial aid probation, they are immediately declared ineligible and must appeal.

### 3F. FINANCIAL AID AND WITHDRAWAL

When a student withdraws after the semester begins, financial aid is prorated based on the withdrawal date. The same weekly schedule as the tuition refund applies:

| Week of Withdrawal | Amount Returned to SU | Amount Retained by Student |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 80% | 20% |
| Week 2 | 60% | 40% |
| Week 3 | 50% | 50% |
| Week 4 | 40% | 60% |
| Week 5 | 30% | 70% |
| After Week 5 | 0% | 100% |

Federal and state programs have their own return-of-funds regulations; SU evaluates compliance with applicable regulations. Written notice of any aid reductions is sent to the student's home address.

Part-time enrollment: Most financial aid and University merit scholarships require full-time enrollment. See Section 3D for exceptions.

### 3I. VETERANS BENEFITS

SU is approved for VA education benefits under Title 38, Chapters 30, 31, 33, and 35.

**Contact:** Veterans' Certifying Official in the Office of the Registrar

**GI Bill Credit:** Veterans who completed basic training and received an honorable discharge are granted one upper-level Fitness and Recreational Activity (FRA) credit upon submitting a DD214 to the Registrar. Active-duty students may submit a Joint Services Military Transcript.

**Enrollment Requirements for VA Benefits:**
1. Complete all admissions requirements as a degree-seeking or visiting student with an approved degree plan
2. Have service schools/experience evaluated by the Registrar for possible military credit
3. Register for courses required for degree completion (with academic advisor)
4. Maintain satisfactory academic progress (both SU academic standing and SAP for financial aid)

**Veterans Benefits and Transition Act of 2018 (Ch. 31 & 33 only):** SU must allow covered individuals to attend class during the period from certificate of eligibility submission until the earlier of: VA payment date OR 90 days after SU certified tuition/fees. SU cannot assess late fees, deny class/library access, or require additional loans for covered individuals experiencing delayed VA disbursement.

**Required Documents for VA Students:** Certificate of eligibility (COE) submitted no later than first day of class; written request to use entitlement; any additional enrollment certification information.

**Note:** VA students are still responsible for the difference between their financial obligation and the VA disbursement amount. Late payment fee policy applies to that difference.

Electronic transcripts available at all times through Self-Service. Official transcripts ordered at: https://www.southwestern.edu/academics/records-registrar/
