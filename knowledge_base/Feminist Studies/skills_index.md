### SU Universal Curriculum Requirements (Paideia Framework)

**Required for ALL degree paths (B.A., B.S., B.F.A., B.Mus., B.S.Ed.):**
- First-Year Seminar (FYS) or Advanced-Entry Seminar (AES): 4 credits
- Languages and Cultures: through third-semester proficiency (up to 12 credits)
- Fitness and Recreational Activity (FRA): 1 credit
- Power and Justice (PJ) course: 1 course, 3–4 credits — **cannot be satisfied by transfer credit**
- Exploration and Breadth: 6 courses from outside the major (18–24 credits), distributed across Fine Arts (FA), Humanities (H), Natural Sciences (NS), Social Sciences (ScS)

- FST04-104 — Introduction to Feminist Studies
- FST04-204 — Feminist Theories and Methods
- Five Area courses including:
  - HIS16-614 Witches, Nuns, Prostitutes, Wives, and Queens (Transregional, satisfies pre/early modern requirement)
  - Two upper-level European area courses
  - One upper-level course from two of four areas (Africa, East Asia, Latin America, United States)

- Students cannot minor in History or Feminist Studies if pursuing this paired major
