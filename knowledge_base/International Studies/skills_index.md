### Majoring & Minoring
- **degree_relevance:** B.A. | Minor
- **description:** This document defines the complete requirements for the International Studies major (9 courses) and minor (5 courses). The major is an integrated major + concentration consisting of five components: (1) one introductory course (ANT35-104, PSC32-144, or any World History HIS16-0X4); (2) three language courses at the 200-level or above beyond the general education requirement (or two in one language plus one spoken at the study abroad location); (3) four track courses from one of three tracks (Global Politics and Economy, Global Society and Culture, or Global Justice), with no more than two courses from a single discipline per track; (4) a mandatory study abroad of at least one semester or one summer (minimum six weeks) in a location where English is not the dominant language; and (5) the capstone seminar (INS38-894), offered each spring, to be taken after study abroad and most track courses. The study abroad policy limits students to no more than three study abroad or transfer courses counting toward the IS major (to comply with SU's 60% residency rule). American Sign Language may not be used to fulfill the language requirement. The minor requires one introductory course, three track courses, and one additional foreign language course beyond the general education requirement (or a fifth track course if placed out), with at least three of five courses at the upper level and no more than two from the same discipline.
- **critical_data:**
  - Major = 9 courses total
  - Minor = 5 courses total
  - Introductory options: ANT35-104, PSC32-144, or any HIS16-0X4 World History course
  - Language requirement (major): 3 courses at 200-level or above beyond the 3-semester gen-ed requirement; OR 2 in one language + 1 in a second language spoken at study abroad location
  - Language proficiency exam required before graduation
  - ASL may NOT fulfill the language requirement
  - Track courses: 4 from one track (Global Politics and Economy, Global Society and Culture, or Global Justice)
  - No more than 2 track courses from a single discipline
  - Study abroad: mandatory; minimum 1 semester OR 1 summer (min. 6 weeks); non-English-dominant location
  - Study abroad programs: choose from approved list (International Studies Committee); non-listed programs require advance approval (one semester ahead)
  - Max 3 study abroad or transfer courses count toward IS major (60% residency rule)
  - Capstone: INS38-894; offered each spring; take after study abroad and most track courses; must relate to student's selected track
  - Minor: 1 intro + 3 track courses + 1 language beyond gen-ed (or 5th track course if placed out); at least 3 of 5 at upper level; no more than 2 from same discipline

### B.A. in International Studies (Integrated Major)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total Courses Required | 9 |
| Minimum GPA (Overall) | 2.000 |
| Minimum GPA (Major) | 2.000 |
| Residency Requirement | 60% of major courses at SU; max 3 study abroad/transfer courses count toward major |
| Source Document | `Majoring___Minoring.txt` |

**Component 1 — Introductory Course (1 course):**
Choose one:
- ANT35-104 Introduction to Cultural Anthropology
- PSC32-144 Comparative Politics
- Any World History course: HIS16-014, 16-024, 16-034, 16-044, or 16-094

**Component 2 — Language Courses (3 courses, all 200-level or above):**

Option A: Three 200+ level courses in the same language
Option B: Two 200+ level courses in one language + one course in a second language spoken at the study abroad location

- All language courses must be beyond the 3-semester general education language requirement (i.e., at least at the 4th-semester/200-level point of study)
- Language proficiency exam required before graduation
- American Sign Language (ASL) may NOT be used to fulfill this requirement

**Component 3 — Track Courses (4 courses from one track):**

Students select ONE track:

**Global Politics and Economy** — focuses on large structures, systems, and institutions. Note: pay close attention to prerequisites for Economics, Political Science, and especially Business courses in this track.

**Global Society and Culture** — focuses on people and communities.

**Global Justice** — courses from either of the above tracks that also carry a Social Justice (SJ) tag.

Rules:
- No more than 2 track courses may be from a single discipline
- Courses not on the approved list may be approved case-by-case by the International Studies Committee

**Component 4 — Study Abroad (mandatory):**
- Minimum: one full semester OR one summer (at least 6 weeks)
- Location: must be a place where English is not the dominant language
- Strongly recommended: programs conducted primarily in a language other than English
- Choose from approved programs (International Studies Committee list) OR obtain advance approval (minimum one semester ahead) for non-listed programs
- Maximum 3 study abroad (or transfer) courses may count toward the IS major
- Students are strongly encouraged to complete study abroad before the capstone

**Component 5 — Capstone (1 course):**
- INS38-894 Capstone
- Offered: every Spring
- Take after study abroad and most track courses
- Project must relate to student's selected track

**Recommended Sequence:**
- Early: Introductory course + begin language sequence
- Mid: Track courses + study abroad
- Final Spring: Capstone (INS38-894)

**Notable Constraints:**
- ASL cannot fulfill the language requirement
- Max 3 study abroad/transfer courses count toward IS major
- No more than 2 track courses from the same discipline
- Study abroad is mandatory — students cannot complete the major without it
- Non-approved study abroad programs require advance committee approval (one semester ahead)
- Capstone project must match chosen track
