### Key Differences Between Degree Paths

- **B.S.Ed. vs. B.A.:** The B.S.Ed. is the certification-bearing degree required for a Texas teaching license. The B.A. is for students pursuing education careers that do not require state licensure. The B.S.Ed. has substantial state-mandated requirements; the B.A. has more elective flexibility.
- **Capstone:** B.S.Ed. capstone is student teaching (70 days, full-time). B.A. capstone is Senior Seminar (40-964).
- **Timeline:** The B.S.Ed. may require an extra semester due to the field-based fall block and student teaching scheduling. The B.A. does not have this constraint.
- **Certification tracks:** Only B.S.Ed. students follow certification tracks (EC–6, 4–8, Secondary, All-Level). B.A. students do not.
- **Music Education:** Students pursuing music teacher certification must major in Music Education, not Education. This is an exception to both degree paths described here.
