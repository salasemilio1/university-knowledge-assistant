### Communication Studies Program Overview
- **degree_relevance:** All
- **description:** Provides a high-level overview of the Communication Studies department's approach and philosophy. Frames the department's core intellectual questions around how messages travel across media, how different mediums (radio, television, the Internet) shape communication, and how institutions maintain and disseminate information. Emphasizes the department's focus on the intersections of communication, culture, and identity. Describes the curriculum as multifaceted and the learning experience as intimate. States the department's goal of providing students a strong critical and theoretical understanding of how social reality is constructed and challenged through communication patterns and practices.

### Majoring and Minoring in Communication Studies
- **degree_relevance:** B.A., Minor
- **description:** Specifies the official requirements for the Communication Studies major (8 courses) and minor (5 courses). Describes the curriculum as organized around two cognate areas — Rhetorical Studies and Critical Media Studies — reflected in the course numbering system (100 = introductory; 200–300 = Rhetorical Studies; 400–500 = bridge; 600–700 = Critical Media Studies; 900 = capstone/tutorials/honors/internships). For the major, five courses are specified (COM75-134, 204, 604, 804, 964) plus three additional COM courses with at least two at upper level. For the minor, COM75-134 is required plus four additional COM courses with at least three at upper level. Notes restrictions on internship (max one counts toward requirements, 4 credits) and independent study (max one counts toward requirements, 4 credits) for both major and minor. ### B.A. in Communication Studies

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total Credit Hours | 127 (SU minimum) |
| Major Courses Required | 8 courses |
| Minimum GPA (Overall) | 2.000 |
| Minimum GPA (Major) | 2.000 |
| Residency Requirement | At least 60% of major credits at SU |
| Source Document | `Majoring___Minoring.txt` |

**Required Core Courses (5 specified):**
- COM75-134 Critical/Cultural Communication Studies — prerequisite for all other core courses; offered Fall and Spring
- COM75-204 Rhetorical Theory — prerequisite: 75-134; offered Spring only
- COM75-604 Critical Media Theory — prerequisite: 75-134; offered Fall only
- COM75-804 Critical/Cultural Research Methods — prerequisites: 75-134, 75-204, 75-604; usually junior year; writing-intensive
- COM75-964 Capstone Research Seminar — prerequisites: 75-134, 75-204, 75-604, 75-804; application required in spring prior to capstone year

**Elective Requirements:**
- Three additional Communication Studies courses (3–4 credits each)
- At least two of the three must be upper level
- One internship (max 4 credits) may count toward the three electives
- One independent study (4 credits) may count toward the three electives
- Additional internship or independent study credits count toward the degree but not toward major requirements

**Additional Graduation Requirements:**
- Capstone application must be submitted in the spring prior to the capstone year — missing this deadline delays graduation by a full year
- All major courses involve significant writing, analysis, research, and creative work

**Notable Constraints:**
- COM75-204 is offered Spring only — failure to take it on schedule delays COM75-804 and COM75-964 by one year
- COM75-604 is offered Fall only — same cascading delay risk
- COM75-804 requires all three prerequisites (134, 204, 604) to be complete
- COM75-964 requires all four prerequisites (134, 204, 604, 804) to be complete
- COM75-154 and COM75-594 Public Speaking do NOT count toward the major or minor
- Only one internship and one independent study count toward major requirements each

### Key Differences Between Degree Paths

| Feature | B.A. Major | Minor |
|---|---|---|
| Number of Courses | 8 | 5 |
| Specified Required Courses | 5 (COM75-134, 204, 604, 804, 964) | 1 (COM75-134 only) |
| Free Elective COM Courses | 3 (at least 2 upper level) | 4 (at least 3 upper level) |
| Capstone Required | Yes (COM75-964; prior spring application) | No |
| Research Methods Required | Yes (COM75-804) | No |
| Rhetorical Theory Required | Yes (COM75-204) | No |
| Critical Media Theory Required | Yes (COM75-604) | No |
| Writing-Intensive COM Requirement | Yes (COM75-804 is WA; COM75-964 is WA) | No formal WA requirement from COM |

The minor requires proportionally more free upper-level electives (3 of 4) than the major (2 of 3), but lacks the required course sequence and capstone of the major.
