HISTORY
Special Opportunities
Every fall, we invite a leading historian to come to campus. This prestigious visitor meets with
majors and minors, leads a discussion in the Historiography course, and gives a public lecture. This
provides our students a wonderful view into how other professional historians work within and
advance their field.
Past Colloquium Visitors:
Jay Winter of Yale University and his work, “100 Years On: Remembering the Great
War, 1914-18.”
David Sowell, “Riots, Schools and Clinics: Writing the History of Medicine in Latin
America”
Todd Shepard, “The Algerian Revolution and the Sexual Revolution in France”
Laura Mitchell, “Watercolors and World History: Art in the Service of Empire”
Franklin Knight, “The Eighteenth Century Revolutions and their Legacies: Two
Hundred Years Later”
Jeffrey Wasserstrom, “Global Shanghai: From Myth to History and Back Again”
Dylan Penningroth, “The Preacher’s Wife: Law, Divorce, and Respectability among
African Americans, 1865-1930”
Monica Green, “The Trial of Floreta d’Ays (1403): Jews, Christians, and Obstetrics
in Later Medieval Marseille”
Clifton Crais, “Heterographies: Writing the Self after the Linguistic Turn”
Patricia Seed
Jeremy Suri, “Power and Protest: Global Revolution and the Rise of Détente”
Dorinda Outram
