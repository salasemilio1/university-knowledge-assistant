### SU Universal Curriculum Requirements (Paideia Framework)

SU's general education model is called **Paideia**. Every major's degree plan is built on top of these shared requirements.

**Required for ALL degree paths:**
- First-Year Seminar (FYS) or Advanced-Entry Seminar (AES): 4 credits
- Languages and Cultures: through third-semester proficiency (up to 12 credits)
- Fitness and Recreational Activity (FRA): 1 credit
- Power and Justice (PJ) course: 1 course, 3-4 credits — **cannot be satisfied by transfer credit**
- Exploration and Breadth: 6 courses from outside the major (18-24 credits)
  - Part I: one course from each of Fine Arts (FA), Humanities (H), Natural Sciences (NS), Social Sciences (ScS)
  - Part II: one additional course from two of the four areas

**Note:** Many History courses carry the (H) Paideia designation (Humanities area). Several also carry (PJ) (Power and Justice) and (SJ) (Social Justice) designations. See Section 4 for course-level details.

   - Students may choose any period or place for their individual research project
   - Paideia: H, WA

**Recommended Sequence:**
- Take World History in first two years (required before Historiography)
- Take several upper-level courses before Historiography
- Historiography typically in junior year (Fall)
- Capstone typically in senior year (Spring)

**Notable Constraints:**
- World History must precede Historiography
- Historiography must precede Capstone
- At least one course must satisfy pre-/early modern requirement
- Students seeking paired majors: check catalog for specific restrictions (cannot minor in either discipline; cannot declare as standard double major)

## SECTION 4 — COURSE INDEX

All courses use the HIS16-XXXX prefix. The first two digits (16) are the department code. Digits 3-4 indicate course level (01-09 = introductory/World History; 11-59 = upper-level; 60 = Historiography; 86 = Capstone; 97 = Honors). The final digit indicates credit hours (4 = 4 credits in most cases, though this should be verified against catalog).
