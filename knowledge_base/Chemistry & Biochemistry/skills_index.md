### Welch Summer Research Program
- **degree_relevance:** All
- **description:** This document describes the department's Welch Summer Research Program, funded continuously since 1985 by the Robert A. Welch Foundation. The program provides equipment and student stipends for an 8-week summer research experience with a faculty mentor. Research areas include medicinal, organic, analytical, environmental, inorganic, and biochemistry. Approximately 10–12 students participate each year. Summer activities include scientific poster preparation training, oral presentation skills, and discussions on ethics in science. Students conclude with a technical report and present a poster at the campus Student Works Symposium the following spring. Applications are submitted to the department chair at FJS 317 and evaluated based on application strength, faculty interview, research interests, training, and coursework.

### Key Differences Between Degree Paths

- **B.A. vs. B.S. (Chemistry):** B.A. requires only 15 major courses and 2 supporting courses (Calc I + Physics I); B.S. requires 17–18 major courses and 6 supporting courses (two semesters each of calculus, physics, plus biology and one elective). B.S. also requires CHE51-704 (Physical Chemistry) which B.A. does not. B.S. is recommended for students intending graduate school in chemistry or related fields.
- **B.S. vs. B.S. ACS-Certified:** ACS-certified track requires 21 courses (vs. 17-18) and mandates specific coverage in all chemistry subdisciplines (inorganic, analytical, biochemistry), adds CHE51-714 (Quantum Mechanics), requires exactly two credits of Methods in Laboratory Research (CHE51-91X, no internship substitution), and requires Department Chair approval for two upper-level electives.
- **Chemistry B.S. vs. Biochemistry B.S.:** Biochemistry B.S. requires both biology lab sequences (BIO50-173/171 AND BIO50-183/181) while Chemistry B.S. requires only one. Biochemistry B.S. requires CHE51-614 and CHE51-624 (both at SU). Biochemistry B.S. allows Statistics instead of Calculus II. Biochemistry B.S. requires CHE51-862 (Advanced Lab Biochemistry) specifically and one upper-level biology cellular/molecular course.
- **Which path for pre-med/health professions:** Either B.S. track is appropriate; Biochemistry B.S. is often chosen by pre-med students due to biochemistry emphasis.
- **Which path for graduate school in chemistry:** B.S. (ACS-Certified) provides the strongest foundation and is recognized by graduate programs nationally.
- **Minor only:** Suitable for students in other majors (e.g., biology, neuroscience, pre-health) who want formal chemistry training but not a full major.
