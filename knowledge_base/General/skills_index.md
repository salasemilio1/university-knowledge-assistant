### 3A. ACCREDITATION, INSTITUTIONAL IDENTITY & ACADEMIC STRUCTURE

**Accreditation:** SACSCOC (baccalaureate degrees), University Senate of the United Methodist Church, and NASM (music programs).

**Three Schools:**
- **Brown College of Arts and Sciences** — Humanities Area (Communication Studies, English, History, Languages/Literatures/Cultures, Philosophy, Religion and Culture) and Social Sciences Area (Economics and Business, Education, Political Science, Psychology, Sociology and Anthropology). Offers B.A., B.S., and B.S.Ed.
- **Garey School of Natural Sciences** — Natural Sciences Area (Biology; Chemistry and Biochemistry; Kinesiology; Mathematics and Computer Science; Physics). 14 majors and minors; B.S. degrees.
- **Sarofim School of Fine Arts** — Fine Arts Area (Art and Art History; Music; Theatre). Offers B.F.A. (Studio Art), B.Mus., and B.A. in art, art history, music, and theatre.

**Interdisciplinary Programs:** Environmental Studies, Feminist Studies, International Studies, Latin American and Border Studies, Race and Ethnicity Studies.

### 3B. THE SOUTHWESTERN UNIVERSITY CURRICULUM (PAIDEIA)

**General Education Philosophy:** Making connections between ways of knowing, between academic coursework and life experiences, and between personal life and world issues. All students participate.

**Required Curriculum Elements (all degrees):**

**1. First-Year Seminar (FYS) or Advanced-Entry Seminar (AES): 4 credits**

**2. Languages and Cultures: up to 12 credits (through 3rd-semester proficiency, XX-164 level)**
Intermediate proficiency (ACTFL). Placement exams offered in Chinese, French, German, Greek, Latin, Spanish. May be fulfilled in languages not offered at SU via transfer or approved placement exam. Heritage languages accepted.

**3. Fitness and Recreational Activity (FRA): 1 credit**
Completing an intercollegiate athletics season satisfies the FRA requirement. Only one FRA credit counts as an elective toward 127 credits.

**4. Power and Justice (PJ): at least one course, 3-4 credits**
SU courses ONLY — cannot be satisfied by transfer credit. Courses marked (PJ) in catalog.

**5. Exploration and Breadth: 6 courses outside the major(s), 18-24 credits**
- Part I: one course each from Fine Arts (FA), Humanities (H), Natural Sciences (NS), Social Sciences (ScS)
- Part II: one additional course from any two of the four areas
- Each course must be 3 or 4 credits (or equivalent — three 1-credit applied music/ensemble courses = one FA course for Part I only; combination not allowed for Part II)

**6. The Major: at least 30 credits (34 for B.S.Ed.)**
At least 60% at SU. No double counting between major and gen ed 30-credit minimums.

**7. The Minor (optional): at least 18 credits**
At least 12 SU credits. Double counting permitted. Maximum two minors.

**8. Capstone Experience:** Every major includes one — determined at the departmental level.

**Writing in the Disciplines (WA):** Integrated requirement; courses marked (WA).

### 3D. GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS

1. Minimum **127 credits** total; at least **64 from SU**; final **32 credits in-residence** at SU
2. At least **60% of major coursework at SU**; minor must include at least **12 SU credits**
3. **Minimum 2.000 GPA** overall AND on all SU coursework
4. **No grade below C-** in major, minor, or area of concentration; **minimum 2.000 average** in major, minor, and area of concentration
5. Students with 64+ transfer credits: up to 16 approved study abroad credits may count for in-residence requirement (does NOT exempt from the 60% major rule)
6. Seniors: up to two courses at another accredited institution with prior approval (after 4 semesters in residence); SAISS study abroad and approved NYAP students exempt from this limit

8. Degrees conferred **December, May, and August only**; Commencement once per year (spring)
9. Seniors may walk with 12 or fewer remaining credits if registered for summer completion
10. Students in financial arrears do not receive diplomas or transcripts until cleared
11. Degree plan requirements expire **six years from end of catalog**

**Second Baccalaureate Degree:**
- 127 credits for first degree + **30 additional in-residence credits**
- Each degree must be a different type AND a distinct major
- Cannot seek same degree type as first degree (e.g., no second B.A.)

### 3H. PASS/D/F POLICY

- Juniors and seniors only
- Maximum **16 total elective P/D/F credits** during junior/senior years
- Cannot replace required courses in major, minor, gen ed, certificates, or University Requirements
- P/D/F-designated courses (permanent): treated as graded for degree requirements; do NOT count toward the 16-credit limit
- Change deadline: end of 5th week (same as drop without record deadline)
- **D or F in a P/D/F course IS included in GPA**; only P is excluded

### 3I. REPEATED COURSES (FALL 2025 NEW RULE)

For course repeats attempted in **Fall 2025 or later:**
- Original grade of **D(+/-) or F:** only the highest grade counts toward cumulative, major, and minor GPA; prior attempt stays on transcript but only in semester GPA
- Original grade of **C- or higher:** all attempts counted in GPA

Credits counted only once unless otherwise specified. Most recent satisfactory completion grants credits on degree plan.

Pre-Fall 2025 attempts: all grades included in GPA (no replacement rule applied retroactively).

### 3J. INCOMPLETE GRADES

- Awarded **only** for medical emergency or equivalent serious circumstances beyond student control
- **Cannot** be given simply to grant more time for coursework
- Must resolve within **4 weeks from the last day of class**; unresolved Incomplete becomes F automatically
- Extension possible via joint petition from student, instructor, and Registrar (new deadline established)
- Incomplete form (with student signature or Center for Academic Success acknowledgement) submitted to Registrar at time of assignment

### 3K. ACADEMIC STATUS — WARNING, PROBATION, DISMISSAL

**Good Standing:** 2.000 cumulative GPA AND at least 24 graded SU credits per academic year.

**Academic Warning (no transcript entry):**
Triggered by: (1) semester GPA below 2.000 while cumulative is at or above 2.000, OR (2) semester GPA above 2.000 but fewer than 24 annual credits completed.
To clear: receive Center for Academic Success counseling; pass at least 12 graded credits next regular semester with 2.20 term GPA; raise cumulative to 2.000.

**Academic Probation (noted on transcript):**
- New students: cumulative GPA below 2.000 at end of second semester, OR fewer than 24 graded SU credits at end of second semester
- Continuing students: cumulative GPA falls below 2.000
To remove: (1) complete PASS Seminar; (2) pass at least 12 graded credits next regular semester with 2.20 semester GPA; (3) raise cumulative GPA to 2.000.
Summer SU courses may count toward standing. Outside courses count toward credit totals only (not GPA; must be pre-approved by Senior Director of Academic Success and Advising).

**Dismissal / Ineligible to Return (noted on transcript):**
Triggered by: (1) fewer than 8 credits in first regular semester; (2) failure of ALL courses in any semester; (3) failure to meet probation removal conditions.
- First dismissal: wait one regular semester
- Second dismissal: wait one year
- Third dismissal: permanent suspension
- After sitting out: must apply for and be granted readmission; readmitted on probation

**Appeal of Academic Ineligibility:** Written appeal to the Registrar by deadline in dismissal letter. Academic Standards Committee decision is FINAL.

### 3L. GRADE APPEAL PROCESS (7 STEPS)

**Three valid grounds for appeal:**
1. Faculty failed to apply stated syllabus weights
2. Prejudicial treatment relative to other students
3. Violation of Academic Rights for Students

**NOT a valid basis:** disagreement with grading difficulty or strictness (if applied equitably).

**Step 1:** Face-to-face conference with faculty member (not email/phone).

**Step 2:** Written communication to instructor; face-to-face meeting with department/program chair (or AVP for Academic Affairs if instructor is the chair).

**Step 3:** Complaint taken to Senior Director of Academic Success and Advising; official form completed. **DEADLINE: 3 calendar weeks from first day of classes of the following regular semester.** Students off campus must work through the Senior Director as liaison.

**Step 4:** Student provides written statement (due 6 calendar weeks from first day of following semester); faculty responds in writing; student may reply. Documents go to Academic Standards Committee.

**Step 5:** Academic Standards Committee reviews in closed session; determines if sufficient grounds exist.

**Step 6:** If grounds found — AVP for Academic Affairs discusses with chair and faculty; faculty develops resolution; grade change reported to Registrar if applicable.

**Step 7:** If faculty refuses committee's recommendation and prejudice/unfairness confirmed — VP for Academic Affairs may authorize grade change to "CR" or "W."

Grade changes by instructor must be initiated within **one calendar year** of end of semester; after one year, changes are impossible.

### 3N. WITHDRAWALS

**Voluntary Withdrawal:** Appointment with Center for Academic Success and Advising required. Allowed through the last day to drop courses. After that date, requires documented medical/emergency and must be done before first day of final exams. Must apply for readmission to return.

**Involuntary Withdrawal:** Weeks 6-10, instructor may request W if excessive absences jeopardize passing. After last day to drop, instructor may assign F for excessive absences at their discretion.

**Retroactive Withdrawal:** After semester completion. Requirements:
- Meet with Senior Director of Academic Success and Advising
- Submit appeal with documentation to Academic Standards Committee
- Must be initiated within **one calendar year** of last day of the semester
- Applies to ALL graded courses (cannot choose individual courses)
- Grades become W; effective from last date of attendance (or 50% mark if unknown)
- Withdrawals before 60% of semester may require repayment of federal, state, and institutional financial aid

### 3O. SPECIAL COURSE OPTIONS

**Independent Study:** Cannot duplicate catalog course; 4 minimum contacts with faculty; not as first course in subject; Independent Project Description Form due by last day to add courses.

**Research Courses:** By special permission; for students of exceptional competence; graduate-school methodology focus.

**Tutorial Courses:** Petition required; rationale for why course cannot be taken normally; weekly meetings with faculty member minimum.

**Academic Internships:** Good standing (2.000 GPA) required; P/D/F basis (unless otherwise specified); part-time during academic year (10-week minimum); full-time in summer (6-week minimum); contact Director of Academic Internships in Center for Integrative Learning.

**Auditing:** No fee for degree-seeking students unless total load exceeds 19 credits (then overload fee). Applied music, ensembles, studio art, and FRA courses may NOT be audited.

- Up to one academic year; one-semester leave may extend to full year; year leave cannot normally be extended
- No readmission application required to return (exception: return from medical leave requires physician certification)
- May be granted for work/study/travel contributing to development, OR personal/medical/financial/health reasons

### 3R. FERPA — STUDENT RECORDS RIGHTS

Students have the right to inspect records within 45 days of request; request amendment of inaccurate records; consent to disclosure; and file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education.

Directory information (may be released without consent): name, addresses, phone numbers, email, date/place of birth, major, activities/sports participation, dates of attendance, degrees/awards, SU ID number, photographs, full/part-time status, most recent prior institution.

Students may restrict directory information by contacting the Registrar. Parents do NOT have automatic access — students must authorize or grant proxy access through the Transact portal (for financial records).

### 3T. TRANSFER CREDIT AND CREDIT BY EXAMINATION

**Transfer Credit:** From regionally accredited institutions; C- or higher (P and CR grades NOT accepted); does not count toward SU GPA. Failure to report = falsification of records.

**AP (entering first-year students, no prior college credit in area):** Scores of 4 or 5; may satisfy gen ed, major, or minor requirements.

**IB (entering first-year students):** Score of 5 or higher on higher-level exams.

**CAPE (entering first-year students):** Scores of 1 or 2 on Unit 1 and Unit 2; Technical subjects excluded.

**No double credit** for presenting both IB and AP/CAPE in the same subject area.

**CLEP (currently enrolled students):** After prior departmental chair approval; CR grade awarded; cannot repeat within 3 months; SU does NOT grant CLEP general exam credit. Foreign language CLEP may be taken before enrollment (credit not posted until SU academic credit is earned).

**Departmental Advanced Standing Exams (currently enrolled):** $50 fee; CR grade for C- or better; cannot be used to repeat a failed course; cannot be taken once enrolled in a continuous sequence; must be taken before last week of classes; results in CR only (may not be accepted by some graduate/professional programs).

**Language Placement:** XX-164 level required (third semester). Placement exams in Chinese, French, German, Greek, Latin, Spanish. Cannot be repeated without department/Registrar approval; may not be taken after starting the language sequence. TOEFL minimum 88 (internet-based); IELTS minimum 6.5 for language requirement exemption.
