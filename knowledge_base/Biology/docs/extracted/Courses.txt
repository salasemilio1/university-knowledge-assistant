Courses
Introductory courses in the Biology Department can serve either as prerequisites for further
study for the biology major/minor or satisfy the Nature Sciences area course requirement for
General Education. The department offers one introductory course intended for non-majors.
Non-introductory courses are designed for students seeking more in-depth information on
cellular and molecular, organismal, and population biology and for students with specific
vocational aims.
See the course catalog for descriptions and updated information.
The Biology Major culminates in the senior capstone experience, where students pursue their
own investigation into a biological question of their choosing. For most students, this capstone
experience will compromise the 4-credit capstone course, taken in the student’s senior year.
Alternatively, students may conduct faculty-mentored research for credit (>3 credits total), then
enroll in the 1-credit capstone course in their senior year. All students who are enrolled in the
1-credit capstone course must complete the 1-hour Capstone Contract form by the end of
the first week of that course. Students who would like to count research mentored by faculty in
another department (ex: chemistry, kinesiology, psychology) towards their capstone experience
in biology must complete the Interdisciplinary Capstone Request for Biology form and receive
approval. (Ideally, this form should be submitted as soon as possible after initiating your faculty-
mentored research experience.)
